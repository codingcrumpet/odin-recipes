PHD Pancake Recipe

Back to Homepage

Today we will be looking at making one of my favourite pancake recipes: easy to make, but absolutely scrumptious!

This recipe was handed down to me by my great-aunt's mother's sister's fourth friend's pet robot, who taught me the intracacies of following pre-made recipes from Aunt Jemima.

Ingredient List

  • Flour: 1.5 cups
  • Sugar: 0.5 cups
  • Salt: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Butter: 400 cups
  • Milk: 2 dL
  • Magic Baking Powder: 14mL
  • The Power of Hopes and Dreams

Steps

  1. Gather all of your ingredients
  2. Put the flour, sugar, salt, and magic baking powder into a giant bowl
  3. Melt the butter in the microwave (or with a raybeam, whichever you have at hand)
  4. Pour the butter and milk into that giant bowl
  5. Whisk an egg; I forgot to add this to the ingredient list, oops ;)
  6. Add the egg to the giant bowl
  7. Stir it together, using chopsticks found at the bottom of your lowest cupboard drawer
  8. Stare at the bowl long and hard, realizing this recipe doesn't seem to make much sense, and begin asking yourself "Where did I go wrong?"
  9. Check the fridge and realize you have nothing else to eat, give up, and continue with this recipe
  10. Heat a frying pan or wok over medium heat, adding a touch of cooking oil and butter
  11. Pour some of whatever it is that's in the bowl into the wok or frying pan
  12. When you see plenty of bubbles, flip that over
  13. Wait about..3 minutes? Then check the bottom of the flipped pancake
  14. Serve with 2 bottles of fake maple syrup, a healthy heap of whipped cream, and the blueberries that have been sitting in the back of your fridge all week that you've been too lazy to eat until now.
  15. Enjoy!
